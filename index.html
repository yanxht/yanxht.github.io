Welcome

This is my personal website, where you can find information on my current research and professional development. I am currently working as a Data Scientist at Azure Compute Insights which builds tools for large-scale cloud infrastructure. For example, our team develops Gandalf, an end-to-end analytics service for safe deployment in Azure.

I received my PhD in Statistics from Cornell University. My research interests focused on using convex optimization techniques to solve structured feature selection problems in high dimensions. After working at Microsoft I became interested in developing link prediction model for large networks at the scale of millions of nodes. I also provide statistical modeling support for non-profit initiatives: collaborating with researchers from Microsoft AI for Good and Seattle Children's Hospital, we publish a study on the geographic variation of sudden unexpected infant death (SUID) across the US.
